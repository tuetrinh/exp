This is an experiment within the ERC Project SPAGAD, Speech Acts in Grammar and Discourse.
It investigates how people communicate in particular situations.

The project is directed by Prof. Dr. Manfred Krifka, Leibniz-Zentrum Allgemeine Sprachwissenschaft (ZAS), Berlin.
You can contact him at krifka@leibniz-zas.de or under the phone number +49-30-20192-401.

The data protection officer of ZAS is Sabine Gotomski, gostomski@ecoprotec.de.
The procedures with respect to the handling of data follow § 27 BDSG for data processing for scientific purposes.

In this experiment, you will read a description of a situation and answer questions about it.
We will then ask you for some details about yourself (for example age and gender).
This is necessary because this information can be important to interpret the data of your experiment.

Your confidentiality will be maintained.
Amazon Mechanical Turk provides no personal information to the requester.
Your data will be stored in anonymous form.
The results of this research study may be presented at meetings or in publications.
The data can be made accessible to other academic non-profit researchers that investigate language or language use on request.

Your participation in the experiment will take about 4 minutes. You have 20 minutes to complete your task.

On completion of the experiment, you will be paid at the posted rate.

Your participation in the experiment is voluntary. You have the right to discontinue participation at any time.

By clicking "I consent", you agree to the following:

1. I am 18 years old or older.
2. I have read the above information, I understand it, and I agree to it.
3. I wish to participate in the experiment.
